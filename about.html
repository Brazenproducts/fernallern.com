About Fern Allern

The Author

Fern Allern has been in love with the fae since she read her first faerie tale at age seven and was deeply unsatisfied that no one fell dangerously in love with the thorn-crowned king in the background.

She writes dark fae romantasy for readers who believe that the most compelling love stories live somewhere between a bargain struck in shadow and the moment you realize you've been wanting the wrong thing all along.

The Thornwood Accord Trilogy is her debut series. Bound by Thorns is the first book — an ~75,000-word slow-burn enemies-to-lovers romance set in a world of warring fae courts, wild magic, and bargains that bind more than just bodies.

She is also working on a companion novella, a prequel story for a character you'll love and then desperately want to rescue, and at least three other series that live rent-free in her head.

Fern is a pen name. The author behind it lives somewhere near the woods (obviously), drinks an embarrassing amount of loose-leaf tea, and has read A Court of Thorns and Roses enough times to recite specific passages unprompted at social events.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is Bound by Thorns releasing?
Soon! Sign up for the newsletter on the homepage to be the first to know the exact release date, plus get access to an exclusive excerpt before launch.
Will the books be available in audiobook format?
Yes! Bound by Thorns will be available on Audible as well as Kindle. Details on the narrator will be announced closer to release.
Is this series part of Kindle Unlimited?
Yes, the plan is to make all Thornwood Accord books available through Kindle Unlimited as well as for direct purchase on Amazon.
How spicy is this series?
The series is written for adult readers and includes mature content. Think dark romantasy in the vein of A Court of Thorns and Roses — slow burn, emotionally intense, with steamy scenes that serve the story.
Are there content warnings?
Yes — full content warnings will be listed on the individual book pages. The series deals with themes including captivity, morally grey characters, fae politics, and death. It is not intended for readers under 18.
